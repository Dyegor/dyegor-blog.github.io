Sprint 5 Core

Core learnings

What are three new things you have learned about yourself and your ego as a result of the core learning?

First of all, I realized that I need to work on my collaboration and improve the ability to deligate tasks. The second thing is, I need to work on my time management (sometimes I really forget to take breaks). Lastly, I will have to practice a lot to master my interpretation skills in order to be able to explain technical things to non-technical users and vice versa.

What are the role of values, empathy and self awareness in learning and programming?

To be successful in programming, like any other job, you need to really love what you do. The road to becoming a good programmer is very long, so you need to understand that this is what you want to do your whole life.

What has surprised you the most about the core learning?

Even familiar things can look different when you look at them from a different angle.

What were the most challenging aspects of the core learning?

Learn to adjust to things that are outside your comfort zone.

Why do you think we, a programming school, are spending so much time focusing on core learning in a web development bootcamp course?

We all have different backgrounds and different personalities, so the core learning is to teach us how to work together efficiently and productively.

Does the time you spent studying core learning here feel like a waste of time? Should you have just used that time to practicing programming instead? Justify your answer.

Not really, I'm interested in learning something else besides the programming itself.

Preping for Bootcamp

What is your long term goal and/or career pathway?

My long term goal is to become a full stack developer, not limited to web applications, but other programming languages like python, C # and many more. I hope to learn the new technical skills that are popular today.

What do you think your biggest strengths and limitations will be in Bootcamp?

My greatest strengths are my conscious choice to become a developer and my passion for programming. I do not have illusions about this, because I understand that the path to my goal will not be easy, but I am ready for it. I don't see any limitation that Bootcamp might have.

What do you think your biggest non-technical challenge at Bootcamp will be?

Teamwork, because we are all newbies and do not know how to work with each other. I just hope that my teammates are reliable and responsible.

What non-technical skills - human skills - would you like to see developed in yourself while at EDA?

Since I've always worked alone, I really want to learn how to split and pass tasks to others.

What are your expectations of yourself on Bootcamp

To gain new skills and knowledge, challenge myself, and become the best version of myself.
